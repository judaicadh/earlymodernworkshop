## Introduction

The expulsion of Jews from Oran (West of Algeria) in 1669 by Spanish occupation has received relatively little scholarly attention, although this event had profound consequences on the Jewish community in North Africa.

From this historical context, our manuscript was written in Judeo-Arabic in majority, and in Hebrew and in Aramaic and in Arabic, the text is from Bodleian Library, it was composed from 90 Muwashah and Zajel.

This text appeared in the middle of the Jewish community in Oran during the Spanish occupation (16 and 17 century) and this manuscript is part of a five- volume named “Mahazor Oran” written by different persons and in a different places (Oran, Mediterranean-Livorno ...), also this manuscript embedded the Sephardic identity and in the Diaspora context in crossing the boundaries of Medieval Mediterranean.

## Source 1 Translation

Bodleian Library, Oxford, Ms. Opp. Add. 4to 85 (Neubauer 1191) cols. 400-303, XC-XCI.

“When we see the sun of the evening, we remember the Land

We remember our home and our country, We remember Oran

What a great Sunday, drumbeats  
Armies and scholars are against us

Give us eight days; We should get out of our country  
We sell our things and our homes, and then the Christians entered

The first Pessa'h night, no table, no chandelier  
No Hagadda, no Hallelujah, no Qadduch, No candle  
And no lamp, No Piyyutim  
At night I remember Oran, We remember our home”

[XCI]

If you saw how They lead us like the sheep

And they beat us

Until we got to our boats

Christians cry out for us

May God afflict them for Ezra and Elijah

And for the Prophet Moses..I remember the homeland

When we saw the sun of Evening, I remember Oran

We remember one night we were in the harbor and they told us to get out

Everyone is crying and asking God to relieve our anguish and give us the wind of the West

Francis Annabi send us a boat  
And protect us from the Turks  
Separate me from the people of my friends  *and the heart is heavy and stingy*

And I left my country  *I travelled in the salty sea*  They are crying and shouting

And a tear from my eye  
We came .we thank God  
Give us the blessing...from the Turks...  
We came to the land of fish and net  
smelly land  
I tell God what this narrowness is  
The sea brought us together

Some of them went to Nice,  
Oh Lord guide us  
How great is the separation!  
O God, give us patience and the best for us

O God in Eretz Israel unite us with Eliahou and the prophets
