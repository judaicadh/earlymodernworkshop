## Introduction

Poetry is the best genre to give voice to the experience of pleasure, so the poet embodies this meeting by words, metaphors, symbols, the space from nature and from the wine. The poet writes about pleasure-seeking from his cultural vantage point, by describing how he can live the pleasure and in which ways he found himself by describing a joyful scene.

rom the first part of the third was to how the poet can taste every moment of life in different spaces in connection with the topic of nature, gardens, the wine, love.

Analysis of this text invites us to explore some of the following questions :

How does pleasure find expression in the poetic repertory especially written in genre of Muwashah ?

What is the significance of language (Judeo-Arabic) to shape the experience of pleasure in different ways?

How can the poetry of pleasure help poets escape the complexity of the historical situation of Jews the Maghreb?

How this text becme an interesting testament to Maghreb Jewish life in western Algeria during the Middle Age?

How text a continuation to a rich Sephardic Nature Love and Wine in a new context?
